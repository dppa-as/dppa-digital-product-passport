## What DPP Means for Retailers & Importers

If you sell, distribute, or import products into the EU or EEA, the Digital Product Passport (DPP) isn't just a compliance issue for manufacturers ‚Äî it directly affects you. Under ESPR and related regulations, retailers and importers will be responsible for ensuring that products on the market display and link to valid product passports.

From electronics and batteries to textiles and furniture, your role is critical in delivering transparent, accessible product data to consumers, regulators, and repair services ‚Äî both online and at the point of sale.

## Common Challenges

- **Fragmented Supply Chain Data** ‚Äî Difficult to verify every product from every supplier includes a valid passport
- **Expanding Legal Responsibility** ‚Äî Expected to ensure DPP compliance before placing products on the EU/EEA market
- **Integration with Retail Channels** ‚Äî DPPs must be reliably connected to products accessible by customers without disrupting systems
- **Limited Time to Adapt** ‚Äî Enforcement timelines moving fast

**Can we link to supplier DPPs instead of hosting them ourselves?**
Yes ‚Äî the platform supports referencing external DPPs via secure URLs, with fallback hosting options.
