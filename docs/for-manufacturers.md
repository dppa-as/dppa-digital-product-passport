## What DPP Means for Manufacturers

If you're manufacturing or branding products sold in the EU or EEA markets, the Digital Product Passport (DPP) will soon become a requirement ‚Äî and an opportunity. Part of the EU's Green Deal and the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR), DPPs are set to transform how product data is shared, verified, and reused across the lifecycle of every product you put on the market.

Whether you produce electronics, textiles, furniture, or consumer goods, the message is clear: you'll need structured, transparent, and lifecycle-based product data ‚Äî at scale.

## FAQ

**Do we need DPPs for all products?**
Not all products are impacted yet. Regulations target specific categories first (batteries, textiles, electronics), but the framework is expanding.

**What data do we need to provide?**
Typical data includes product identifiers, material composition, carbon footprint, repairability, safety info, and recycling instructions.

**Can we connect our PIM system?**
Yes. The platform integrates with PIM systems like Akeneo, PimCore, and inRiver. You can also start with Excel-based submissions.

**Who owns the data?**
You do. We never claim ownership. Export in JSON anytime ‚Äî no lock-in.
